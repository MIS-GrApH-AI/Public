Medical Knowledge Graph Issues

ES version 2021-08-08 /

  • Introduction:
    • Graph technologies are expected to be useful in the following objectives:
      • Decision support, currently our first priority,
      • Research,
      • Education.
    • Therefore a medical knowledge database is necessary:
      • Although the requirement for decision support are not yet exactly known, but anyhow graph allowing easy access and navigation in a large space of standardized medical knowledge.
    • Current objectives:
      • (1) Definition of the models suited for the above objectives.
      • (2) Find source of information and procedure to convert available information into graphs.
    • Such a knowledge base include 3 aspects, concepts, relations between concepts and "clinical modifiers".
  • ( A ) Concepts:
    • At first sight it looks that many definitions of concepts are already available in diverse classification systems.
    • A database of concepts must include at least complaints, symptoms, examinations, problems or diagnoses, treatment, etc...
    • There is a need for an unified way to identify concepts, as UMLS.
    • Historically most existing classification systems have bee created for epidemiological purposes, but it is not certain that our requirements for decision support will be completely covered. In general epidemiological systems work on large populations while the information needed for the care of individual patients could be a little different. In epidemiology many information are simply seen. as true or false without any gradation. The ICD created about 100 years ago was intended for processing with punch cards.
    • Sources of concepts:
      • The big classifications from UMLS, LOINC, pharmacology, etc... to be converted in graphs.
    • Remark: relationships are anywhere, at the end definition of concepts are explained as links to other concepts. In any popular dictionary words are described by means of other words i.e. links to other concepts.
  • ( B ) Relationships between concept:
    • Precise information about relationships between  concepts will be less easy to find.
    • Main types of relations in 2 directions:
      • From Symptoms to Problems
      • From Problems to associated Symptoms
      • From Problem to recommendations of further examinations.
      • From Problem to recommended treatments.
      • From Treatment to Problems, including unwanted side effects.
    • Sources of relationships:
      • Courses intended for medical students.
      • Textbooks, for example Harrison.
      • Research based on large population of patient like MIMIC could help to quantify relationships.
      • Expertise from very qualified medical professionals.
      • Exercises of graphs on a white board in front of a group of students.
    • Format:
      • Conversion of free text. The relations should be quantified, many adjectives should be converted into coefficients.
  • ( C ) "Clinical modifiers":
    • Clinical modifiers are essential for both concepts and relations.
    • Generic attributes:
      • Degree of belief (by the author)
      • Intensity, severity,
      • Frequency,
      • Degree of trust in the source of information.
      • Importance, for example if life threatening issue or simple disagreement.
      • ....
    • Attributes: of concepts
      • Main types of concepts:
        • Observation, i.e. the facts.
        • Problems, synthesis of a set of facts, hypothesis, diagnose.
        • Actions, exploratory and therapeutic actions.
    • Attributes of relations:
      • Causal or simple co-occurence.
      • Relation weight:
        • Relative weights of relations are essential in clinical decisions.
        • This weight could be represented as coefficients from 0. to 1. For example weak relations as 0.5 or fully valid relations as 1.0
      • Exclusion or negative relations:
        • Could be seen a separate type of relation ?
      • Main relations types, provisional brain storming list:
        • ASSOCIATED_WITH
        • SUGGEST
        • EXCLUDE
        • RECOMMAND
        • CAUSED_BY
        • CONSEQUECE_OF
        • INCOMPATIBILITY:
        • RESPONSIBLE_FOR
        • INITIATED
        • ASSOCIATED_DURING_VISIT
        • ASSOCIATED_WITH
        • MEASUREMENT_OF
        • ...
